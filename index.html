Things I give a shit about even if you don't

It's not that your bad people, your just don't know any better. Thats why i feel it's important to educate clients about being good citizens of the Internet. Here are a few things I things I always keep in mind even if it's not an obvious requirement of the project.

Accessibility

Not everyone is able to view your site with a traditional browser. I always build sites to be accessible for vision impaired users by supporting popular screen readers such as ariea, and jaws.

Clean Simantic and maitanable Markup

I think the haml guys say it best, Markup Should be Beautiful. Markup should not be used merely as a tool to get browsers to render a page how the author wants it rendered. The rendering isn't the only thing people have to see; they have to see, modify, and understand the markup as well. Thus, the markup should be just as user-friendly and pleasant as the rendered result.

no frameworks

Every site is its own thing, handmade from scratch. the web is an ever changeing medium, i always playing with new techneques and technologys, in part becouse it nessisary to stay current. but also becouse its more fun that way.
